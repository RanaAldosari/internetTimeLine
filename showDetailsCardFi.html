2000-until now
internet

With the beginning of the new millennium, a new type of online communication emerged: social networking sites. It began with sites like MySpace (2003), followed by Facebook (2004) and Twitter (2006). These platforms have become an essential part of people's lives, a way to communicate, express themselves, and even shop.With the advancement of smartphones, especially after the release of the first iPhone in 2007, the internet became available anytime, anywhere. Users no longer needed a computer to connect; the phone became a means of browsing, taking pictures, working, and playing.Entire markets have emerged online, such as e-commerce, digital services, distance learning, and freelancing. Platforms like Zoom, Udemy, and Freelancer have changed the concept of work and study.
