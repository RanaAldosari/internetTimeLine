How did the Internet appear?

The Internet emerged in 1969, when the US Department of Defense established a communications network linking a number of analysis centers and satellites, dubbed ARPANET. These networks subsequently connected numerous universities and research institutions, with the goal of maximizing the computational capabilities of available computers.Access to ARPANET expanded in 1981 when the National Science Foundation (NSF) funded the Computer Science Network (CSNET). In 1982, the Internet Protocol Suite (TCP-IP) was introduced as the standard networking protocol on ARPANET. The NSF also funded national supercomputing centers at several universities and provided interconnections with the NSFNET project, which also established network access to supercomputer sites in the United States for research and educational organizations.
