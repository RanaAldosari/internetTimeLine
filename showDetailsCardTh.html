How did the Internet spread and become a life network?

Internet use remained limited to universities and academic centers until 1991, when the Internet's most significant turning point occurred. The network expanded and progressed, and university students began contributing their information until the Mosaic browser was introduced, ushering in the expansion of networks around the world.Thirty years ago, researchers at CERN introduced the World Wide Web (www) on April 30, 1993, and the Internet began to spread widely. Web technology has changed little since then. Beginning in 1994, there was a significant increase in public interest in the Internet, and by 1996, the term "web" had become commonplace. Consequently, the technology spread to its current state. We now hear about the "Internet of Things," social media, and many other inventions that emerged from the "mother web."
