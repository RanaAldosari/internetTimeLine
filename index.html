1969-1982
How did the Internet appear?

The Internet emerged in 1969, when the US Department of Defense established a communications network linking a number of analysis centers and satellites, dubbed ARPANET. These networks subsequently connected numerous universities and research institutions, with the goal of maximizing the computational capabilities of available computers.

How did the Internet spread and become a life network?

Internet use remained limited to universities and academic centers until 1991, when the Internet's most significant turning point occurred. The network expanded and progressed, and university students began contributing their information until the Mosaic browser was introduced, ushering in the expansion of networks around the world.
