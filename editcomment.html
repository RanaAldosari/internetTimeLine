How did people communicate before the Internet?

Throughout human history, sending a message to someone required effort, such as sending someone or shouting, to reach neighbors. Another solution was necessary. Since the first century BC, people began raising carrier pigeons, thanks to their innate ability to find their way home over long distances.

Methods used in ancient communication:
  • Carrier pigeons were used in some periods to carry messages over long distances.
  • People would visit each other face to face to talk and exchange news.
  • In the 20th century, landline telephones became a popular means of voice communication between people, especially in cities.
  • Radio and television are two means of broadcasting news and information.
  • Newspapers and magazines were an important means of conveying news, advertisements, and articles, and were distributed daily or weekly.
